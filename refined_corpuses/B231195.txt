(smith v. robbins (2000) 528 u.s. 259, 277-284 [120 s.ct. 746, 145 l.ed.2d 756]; people v. kelly (2006) 40 cal.4th 106; people v. wende (1979) 25 cal.3d 436, 441.) a criminal defendant who appeals following a plea of no contest or guilty without a certificate of probable cause can only challenge the denial of a motion to suppress evidence or raise grounds arising after the entry of the plea that do not affect the use no suppression motion was filed in this case, and rodriguez is, in essence, attacking the validity of his plea without a certificate of probable cause, his notice of appeal is inoperative. the appeal must be dismissed.
