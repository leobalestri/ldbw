appearance for plaintiff and respondent. ___________________ 2 on february 16, 2016, at approximately 9:10 p.m., pablo lopez (lopez) entered the home depot located at 7870 van nuys boulevard. he selected expensive items from the shelves, including two paint sprayers and a chainsaw, totaling $817. a loss prevention agent, michael trejo, watched lopez place the items in a shopping cart and make his way to a cash register near the exit. lopez hung back for a moment, making it appear he was going to pay, before walking past the registers and leaving the store. lopez then rode the shopping cart away from the exit, pushing it with one foot. trejo yelled, hey, stop himself as a loss prevention agent. as trejo approached lopez, approximately 20 yards from the exit, lopez threw a sprayer, which weighed about eight to 12 pounds, at trejo. although initially stunned, trejo was able to catch up with lopez. trejo then grabbed and the shopping cart in order to detain him. with his left arm, lopez elbowed trejo, hitting him directly on the neck and chest in an attempt to escape with the stolen items. lopez on the ground and managed to get his arms around lopez. lopez then tried, unsuccessfully, to bite trejo on the arm. trejo struggled with lopez and ultimately placed lopez in handcuffs. during the struggle, lopez managed to flip trejo, causing trejo to hit his head on the ground, which resulted in an abrasion. trejo suffered several other injuries as a result of the struggle, including pain to his whole body, numbness in his right arm, and wrist pain. 3 at trial, a jury convicted lopez trial, lopez of second degree robbery in violation of penal code sections 211 and 212.5, subdivision (c). the trial court sentenced lopez to the low term of 24 months in prison. lopez received 156 days of credit for time served and was ordered to pay a $40 court security fee, a $30 administrative fee, a $30 facilities assessment fee, a $300 parole restitution fine and a $300 parole restitution fine. lopez timely filed a notice of appeal but did not identify any grounds for relief. we have examined the entire record counsel fully complied with his responsibilities and that no arguable issues exist.
