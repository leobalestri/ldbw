(see smith v. robbins (2000) 528 u.s. 259, 277-284.) on february 2, 2016, we advised defendant that he had 30 days within which to personally submit any contentions or arguments he wished us to consider. no response has been received. we have examined the entire record and are satisfied appointed appellate counsel has fully complied with her responsibilities. we asked the parties to brief the question whether defendant received correct amounts of presentence custody and conduct credit. we conclude that he did. we base defendant was arrested on october 3, 2014, posted bail on october 7, 2014, and remained free of custody until after he was sentenced. as noted above, the oral pronouncement sentence states defendant was entitled to five days in presentence custody plus four days for good conduct. the award was correct. (people v. adams (2016) 245 cal.app.4th 498, 508 [custody credit]; people v. whitaker (2015) 238 cal.app.4th 1354, 1357-1362 [conduct credit].) the december 26, 2014 minute order is incorrect insofar as it states that defendant posted bail on october 14, 2014. 3 the judgment is affirmed. not to be published in the official reports turner, p.j. we concur: kriegler, j. baker, j.
